# Humanization QA Checklist

Use this checklist after drafting or rewriting.

## Pass conditions

- transitions do not read like a mechanical outline
- sentence lengths visibly vary
- paragraph lengths visibly vary
- abstract claims are supported by examples, images, or actions
- tone matches the style DNA
- platform formatting matches the target channel
- the article contains judgment, not only neutral explanation

## Common red flags

- too many formulaic transitions
- same-length paragraphs across the whole article
- every paragraph follows topic sentence plus explanation plus mini-summary
- too much abstraction and not enough scene detail
- slang added everywhere in an unnatural way
- fake personality fillers without real viewpoint

## Revision moves

### If the text feels too robotic

- break one long formal sentence into two uneven lines
- replace one abstract sentence with a concrete scene
- add one honest judgment or reaction

### If the text feels too obviously edited

- remove overdone slang
- remove stacked metaphors
- smooth abrupt rhythm jumps

### If the text does not sound like the target author

- compare with style DNA
- restore point of view
- restore sentence rhythm
- restore preferred explanation pattern

## Simple scoring

Score each item from `1-5`:

- tone consistency
- sentence rhythm
- paragraph rhythm
- specificity
- reader intimacy
- platform fit

Treat any item below `3` as a required revision.
